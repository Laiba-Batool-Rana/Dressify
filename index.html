A-Line Dress:

A classic silhouette that is fitted at the bodice and flares out gradually to create an "A" shape, often flattering on various body types.

Wrap Dress:

Characterized by its wrap-around style, featuring a front closure formed by wrapping one side of the dress across the other and tying at the waist, creating a flattering V-neckline.

Shift Dress:

A simple, straight-cut dress that hangs loosely from the shoulders down to the hem, typically without a defined waistline, offering a relaxed and versatile fit.

Sheath Dress:

A form-fitting dress that skims the body's curves without being too tight, typically featuring a tailored silhouette with a defined waist and a straight skirt.

Fit and Flare Dress:

Combines a fitted bodice with a flared skirt, accentuating the waist and creating an hourglass shape. The skirt flares out from the waist down, often featuring pleats or gathers for added volume.

Maxi Dress:

A floor-length dress that flows down to the ankles or beyond, offering a relaxed and elegant look, often favored for formal occasions or summer gatherings.
